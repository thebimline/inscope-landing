Many projects suffer from an information structure problem.

Valuable project information is locked in PDF reports making it harder to action.
Construction receives a programme timeline, while the project and finance do not.
Project goals, due diligence risks and team responsibilities are not systematised or tracked.
Unidentified information gaps lead to delays when they block start.
Spreadsheets, reports, emails and specialist tools rarely form a coherent view of the project.
Teams rely on reports assembled from fragmented project information with limited visibility of the underlying operational activity.
Performance does not easily feed back into planning and risk avoidance.

The result is more time spent managing information and less time managing the project.

Success is planned and managed.

The Information Worksite is the answer to the information structure problem. It helps create the project roadmap and information infrastructure needed for success. It encourages teams to define what matters, track it and understand significance. It allows teams to combine the financial timeline and project programme into one delivery timeline to track how performance affects financial outcomes.
